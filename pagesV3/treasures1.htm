Holy Spirit Parish Treasures (pg 1)

Holy Spirit has cultivated a rich and diverse collection of Statuary , Icons, Relics and Tapestries. In this section of the web site are a collection of references to those who would like to know more about the church treasures and what they signify. The articles by Father Fred began appearing in the Church Bulletin in January 2008.

January 13, 2008
Beginning today, I am going to try to write for as long as it takes a brief article under the above heading to call attention to the fixtures and art works we have in our church. Because many of you are new parishioners who do not know our history and because some of you may be just unaware of the meaning of things we use and see regularly in church, I hope these articles will help your worship and devotion as you make better use what is provided. Let me know after a few articles if this is helpful or suggest topics. As a starter, I ask if you realize as you enter church the significance of walking past the Baptismal Font? It should remind you every time of YOUR Baptism and that because of that Baptism you have the right and privilege to be in church as part of the Body of Christ – his new presence in our world through you. As you leave past the font, you are reminded of your responsibility to BE CHURCH (Christ) in the world outside. Blessing yourself with holy water reinforces this message.

January 20, 2008
Best place to start, I suppose, is at the beginning. Before you enter the church – at least during the growing season – the gardens are supposed to create an attitude of prayer, peace and beauty re-enforced by the outdoor shrines of the Sacred Heart of Jesus, Our Lady, and St. Jude. Also, in summer, St. Elizabeth of Hungary and St. Francis of Assisi rest in the gardens in front of the rectory.
As you approach the main doors, you pass the image of Our Lady of Guadalupe on one side indicating we are a strong .Right to Life advocacy. parish. On the other side is St. Michael the Archangel whom we ask to be with us as we go into the world to overcome the forces of evil.
These are all obvious, but we just renewed the Epiphany Blessing over the main doors. This is not so easily seen but says .20+C+M+B+08. indicating the current year, the four seasons or directions and the names of the three Magi by giving their initials. C-M-B can also mean may Christ bless this house. You can put the same blessing over your main door. Do you know the names of the Magi? I will tell you next week.

January 27, 2008
Do I need to tell you the names of the Magi? Just in case you have forgotten, they are: Casper, Melchior and Balthasar. Before you go through the doors over which the Epiphany Blessing is placed, you are in what we call the Gathering Area. Here you find important literature like old bulletins you may have missed, newsletters, the pamphlet rack, bulletin board, a showcase for items from St. John’s Store that you can order, children’s table where they will find pages to color relating to the day’s Liturgy, a very interesting Rosary in a frame (take a close-up look).

We have chairs and speaker broadcasting the mass for those who need to be away from the body of the church. At the Fatima table you find pictures to inspire our kids of the youngest persons to be declared .Blessed. - Jacinta and Francisco Marto to whom Our Lady of Fatima appeared. Leaves from the tree where she appeared are under their pictures. We await a custom storage cabinet in the back hallway to make that area neater. There is still more in this area I will mention next week

.


February 3, 2008
The gathering area which I began to describe last week is not that large but has more in it than I realized myself. So I continue. In the southwest corner is a shrine to St. Joseph with a statue I found in an antique shop some years ago. He watches over the little ones who gather there just as he watched over the Christ child.

Pictures of the former pastors and the parish council are found here. Perhaps after our new pictures are taken, I will be added. Note the first two pastors were Father Joseph Lynch and Father Joseph Daily. The second two pastors are Father Fred Bush and Father Fred Helfrich. Four pastors and two first names. On this same wall is an interesting cross with symbols of lent which comes from the early days of the parish – a hand crafted gift of the first grade religion class of 1968 – forty years ago! Two other pictures are by the pamphlet rack – our current Pope Benedict XVI and Father Feeney who assisted here in his retirement. The Holy Water container is available here for those needing Holy Water. Next to it is usually some display relating to the season or an upcoming or current feast to help set the scene. before you enter the church.

February 10, 2008
While there may still be a couple things I omitted in the Gathering Area, it is time to move into the church. Usually people enter by the North doors so we have retained the custom of placing a host in the ciborium if you are receiving Communion. Thus, even if you don‘t actually bring the Gifts to the altar, you are reminded that you are putting yourself in there – your life- so that when it becomes the Body and Blood of Jesus you, too, will be changed. As a stewardship parish, this simple action represents the gift of your time, talent and treasure.

However, before you get to that table, you will first encounter, just inside the door on your right, a wax copy of an ivory ninth century carving depicting .Christ in majesty.. This was a gift to me from a parish family (Francis Cardinal George, Archbishop of Chicago also has one!) I put it at the entrance to remind us that it is our job to build the Kingdom of God by making Christ the King and Center of our lives. That is why we have come. Over the holy water font is a cross depicting various .holy spirit. experiences indicating you are entering a church dedicated to the Holy Spirit. I previously explained the holy water.

February 17, 2008
Many of you know that I have an extensive collection of icons, some of which have metal covers (called rizas) and which I bring to church at the proper times. I am happy to be able to provide anything which can aid or further your devotion and prayer. So you need to be always on the lookout for .visiting treasures.. From this collection, however, I have chosen a few items to be permanently in the south entrance to the church on the other side of the sacristy from the entrance.
This is what I call the .Marian Corridor.. Four icons of Our Lady are here. Because we have a significant number of people of Polish and Lithuanian background, the two images of Mary, most popular in Poland and Lithuania, are here – Our Lady of Czestochowa and Our Lady of Mercy of Vilnius. The third icon is the popular Our Lady of Kazan which represents my close connection to the local Russian parish. On the opposite wall is a huge icon print of the Dormition (Falling Asleep) of the Virgin which is brought into the church for the August 15 Feast of the Dormition and Assumption of Our Lady. If you never use this entrance/exit, make a special visit to explore these images.
