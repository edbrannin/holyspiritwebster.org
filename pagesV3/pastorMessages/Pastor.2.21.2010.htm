Alive in the Spirit ... 2/21/2010

   
 

 

Lent has begun! You should have your Lenten letter from me by now. Keep the list of suggested ways to observe lent where you can consult it regularly. Save in a safe place the Holy Week schedule and discuss as a family (when applicable) how you will fill the Lenten sacrificial envelope.. Everyone should contribute some money saved from something you didn't buy (your sacrifice.) and put it into your Lenten jar. Then, at the end of Lent combine it all into a bill or check you can put into the envelope. This is in itself an act of charity saving the money.counters much grief.
As you probably know, those who follow the Julian calendar (ours is Gregorian) both Orthodox and some Eastern Church Catholics also usually have a different day for Easter (and, therefore, Lent as well) since the feast has to be after Passover. However, this year the date for Passover is such that there is no delay in celebrating Easter. Thus all of us . East and West . are celebrating Lent and Easter at the same time.

That means that this is the First Sunday in Lent for all of us. While our focus on the First Sunday is Jesus in the desert being tempted and the call for us to enter the symbolic desert of Lent by eliminating distractions, the Eastern Church keeps this day as the Festival of the Restoration of the Holy Icons or Sunday of Orthodoxy . the triumph of the true faith regarding the use of holy images. During Lent we try to become better icons. of Christ reflecting him in our world.

 

In Rochester it has long been the custom for the various Orthodox (not in union with Rome) parishes to gather together on the First Sunday of Lent to celebrate Vespers with its procession of icons. For many, many years I have attended this and hope to do so tonight (Sunday). Since it would be difficult to get back on time for our Night Prayer and Benediction, I have cancelled Solemn Adoration and the evening prayers today, Sunday February 21st. The church will be open for those who wish to keep their hours but no group prayer at 8:45PM. On successive Sundays I encourage you to gather for Night Prayer.

Saints‘ celebrations go low key during Lent but some Feasts are celebrated fully including Monday‘s Feast of St. Peter‘s Chair – highlighting the position of authority and unity St. Peter had and now held by successive Popes. It is a day to renew our loyalty to our Holy Father, Pope Benedict XVI. The early Christian martyr Polycarp is remembered on Tuesday. On Thursday some celebrate the Benedictine Abbess Walburga famous for many miracles. The young Passionist (of whom Father Peter and I have relics) St. Gabriel of Our Lady of Sorrows has the spotlight on Saturday.


Then we go from the desert to the mountain for the Transfiguration next Sunday. Have a grace-filled Lent.
Father Fred
