Alive in the Spirit ... 2/28/2010

   

 

 

As you know, each year after Easter the Bishop gathers all the priests actively serving in our Diocese to the Annual Convocation. The featured speaker this year, who will focus on our spiritual renewal as we approach the climax of our Diocesan Spirit Alive renewal on Pentecost, will be Father Ronald Rolheiser, OMI. As we begin the month of St. Joseph this week I thought these comments from him would be appropriate for inspiration and meditation. I am taking them from the December 23, 2009 issue of North Country Catholic.
In his efforts to grapple with the difficult situation of finding Mary "with child", Father Rolheiser says:

He shows how a person can be a pious believer, deeply faithful to everything within his religious tradition, and yet at the same time be open to a mystery beyond both his human and religious understanding.


So when God breaks into one's life in new, unimaginable ways we can look to St. Joseph to guide us in how to respond. Again, he says:

In essence what Joseph teaches us is how to live in loving fidelity to all that we cling to humanly and religiously, even as we are open to a mystery of God that takes us beyond all the categories of our religious practice and imagination.

 

St. Joseph of so long ago can still help us as we try to live our faith in a world of surprises. St. Teresa of Avila calls him "our helper in every need".

On Monday we thank St. David, king and Patron of Wales for all the beautiful Welsh hymns that enhance our Liturgies. Wednesday we give thanks for another American Saint . Katherine Drexel . who gave of her own fortune to serve and provide for the needs of America's poorest.

The Polish and Lithuanians rejoice in honoring St. Casimir on the 4th. His favorite hymn was "Daily, Daily, Sing to Mary". We close off the week with the early martyrs Felicitas and Perpetua who still inspire us in our quest for "perpetual felicity" and St. Colette.


That is all I have for now. May your Lent and March be fruitful in helping you be Alive in the Spirit.
Father Fred
