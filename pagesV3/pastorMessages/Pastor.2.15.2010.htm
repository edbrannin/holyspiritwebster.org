Alive in the Spirit ... 2/14/2010

   
 

 

It's here! Right around the corner. We move from the chocolates and roses of Valentine¡¥s Day to the parities of Mardi Gras to the ASHES which signify the beginning of Lent ¡V the Church¡¥s annual time of Retreat and Renewal preparing us for the spiritual springtime of Easter. If you did not yet receive your Lenten mailing, it should arrive by Monday.

Please read the letter together as a family (when applicable) keep the list of ways to observe Lent to consult throughout the period, use your sacrificial envelope (again as a family involving the children when applicable) and make this the best preparation for Easter of your whole life.


Since Ash Wednesday comes during winter break this year, hopefully this will allow more children and families to observe opening day. The 17th would ordinarily be the Memorial of the Seven Holy Founders of the Servite Order. We also miss the Feast of the great Apostles to the Slavs, Saints Cyril and Methodius (which is also St. Valentine's Day) on the 14th day of February because it is a Sunday. The confessor of Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque ¡V Saint Claude de la Colombiere ¡V is remembered on Monday, the 15th.


Then comes Fat Tuesday (Mardi Gras), the last day to feast before the fast. Ash Wednesday is a day of fast and abstinence. The artist, Bl. Fra Angelico is honored on the 18th. Saturday is blessed as the Feast of the children of Fatima the youngest persons to be beatified Blessed Francisco Marto and Blessed Jacinta Marto reminding us that even as children we are called to be saints.

 

 

In addition to being Valentine Weekend, February 13/14 is Public Policy Weekend and the subject for advocacy is the crucial but controversial problem of comprehensive immigration reform. Bishop Clark's letter regarding this issue reminds us that Jesus himself was an immigrant in Egypt seeking asylum and in his public ministry did not have an established home.

He continues Our church recognizes both the right of countries to establish border controls and the right of individuals to migrate in order to live a life of human dignity. As we work towards trade agreements that allow people to support themselves in their countries of origin, we need to convince lawmakers to enact immigration reform that:

  • Provides an earned path to citizenship for those who have been living and working in our country.
  • Protect families and encourages reunification
  • Has legal pathways for temporary workers to come and work in a safe, humane and orderly manner and be able to return to their country of origin, and
  • Restores due process protections for immigrants.


For those who are ready to advocate for reform, please consider participating in the February postcard campaign.

These postcards are available in the gathering area. Wishing you a holy and happy Lent full of grace and blessings,
Father Fred
