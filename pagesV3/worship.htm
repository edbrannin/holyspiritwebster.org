Holy Spirit Eucharistic Adoration

We are privileged to have Eucharistic Adoration here in this parish. It is only through the dedication of people who have committed one hour of their time and through the support of our priests that this is possible.
Often we have only one person committed to a specific hour. Some adorers have taken an extra hour or are substituting to help fill this gap. Please think about this. Pick up the phone and make that commitment. Call Joan Poore at 377-3463


Reverence Reminder


  • Be sure the ringers on cell phones or pagers are turned off before entering the church.
  • Our arrival time and first gestures should reflect reverence.
  • Try to be timely. It is distracting when people arrive to Mass late.
  • When in the pew, you should quietly pray to yourself or meditate. If you must talk, make it brief and whisper.
  • After communion, it is appropiate to remain kneeling until the priest returns to his seat.
  • The final part of Mass is singing a last hymn. It is courteous to stay until the hymn is over.
