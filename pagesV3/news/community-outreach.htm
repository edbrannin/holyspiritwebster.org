Need Pregnancy Help?
The Focus Pregnancy Help Center at 86 University Ave offers a safe, non-judgmental haven for women and girls. We provide free information and assistance to all pregnant women, including pregnancy testing, health and financial advice, counseling and referrals to the vast network of support services available within the Rochester area for expectant moms. The Center offers alternative to abortions. If you need our help, please call 218-4051.

Assistance for the Elderly
Do you need extra help to stay living in your own home, or, do you know someone who does? The Expanded In-home Services for the Elderly Program (EISEP) has funds available to help pay for personal care and housekeeping aide service. Work with experienced case managers to come up with a plan to keep you independent. To be eligible you must live in Monroe County, be over age 60, have a need for help and not receive Medicaid assistance. Please contact Catholic Family Center at 262-7060 to learn more

 

The Cathedral Community and the Webster-Penfield Catholic Connection
A new partnership!


The Cathedral Community, comprised of three newly merged parishes in northwest Rochester, and the four parishes of the Webster-Penfield Catholic Connection are forming a partnership that will strengthen our commitment to prayer, worship, friendship and ministry.
As a first initiative, the Webster-Penfield parishes will join the Cathedral Community in its recently begun ministry (clothing distribution, tutoring and assistance with an urban farm) to refugees from Burma, now living in the neighborhood. In time, we may work together in other urban (or suburban) ministries.
But the goal of the partnership is broader than outreach ministry. It is hoped that it will include shared liturgies, an open invitation to events in each other‘s parishes, social events, and a general coming-together‘ to help us understand each other‘s needs as city and suburban communities — and to help us all better experience the universal church. For more information contact: Kim Haynes 586-7693 and kdhaynes@frontiernet.net,

 

Can You Spare Some Time?
Tutors are needed to assist the Burmese refugees at Mary's Place as they struggle to learn English. Ro arrives early for her one-hour tutoring session and hates to leave when she is finished. She often stays around afterwards to help sort and fold the donated clothing. During her session, she may try reading from simple news articles or a chapter from Sarah, Plain and Tall.. She writes down unknown words to learn their definition. There are many others like Ro who need tutoring. If you can help, please contact Kathy LaBue, (585) 278-0835, marysplace@frontier.com. Sessions are at the convenience of the tutor during Mary's Place hours; Tues., 1-5 p.m., Thurs. 10-5 p.m., and Sat., 10-2 p.m.

Life at the Poverty Line
Nearly 37 million people in the United States live below the poverty line, and 13 million of those are children. The Catholic Campaign for Human Development (CCHD) works to create lasting solutions to the problem of poverty through real community change.
Visit www.povertyusa.org to learn more.

The Price of Freedom is Visible Here
The VA Medical Center at Canandaigua and the Rochester VA Outpatient Clinic are proud to serve those who have served, but many Veterans who are eligible for VA health care may not know about their benefits. Enrollment in the VA health care system provides a comprehensive benefits package, often at low or no cost to the Veteran. If you know a Veteran in need of health care, encourage him/her to call today to check on the latest information about eligibility/benefits information in the Veterans Service Center. The Canandaigua VA Medical Center number is (585) 393-7348 and for Rochester VA Outpatient Clinic the number is (585)463-2687
