LITURGY

Liturgy Committee - This new committee will develop and maintain a program of liturgical worship, working in conjunction with the PASTOR, Rev. P. Frederick Helfrich the PASTORAL ASSOCIATE Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317, and the DIRECTOR OF MUSIC Gabriel Pellegrino 671-7933 . Responsibilities will include recruitment, training, and evaluation of lectors (commentators), Communion Ministers, ushers, altar servers, and sacristans. Consideration should also be given to the Rite of Christian initiation of Adults (RCIA), other special worship programs, music resources, and the Church environment.

The pastor, pastoral associate, and director of music are members of the Liturgy Committee. Other members should include experienced Communion Ministers, lectors, musicians, ushers, and sacristans.

All members of the Liturgy Committee should have a working knowledge of the principal liturgical documents of the Church, including pertinent sections of the Revised Code of Canon Law, the Constitution on the Sacred Liturgy, and the General Instruction of the Roman Missal. The Liturgy Committee with also work with the Faith Formation Committee to instruct the faithful in matters liturgical.

The liturgy committee will also work to promote, encourage and coordinate the growth of spiritual life with special devotions (Advent and Lenten devotions, Pentecost novena, May devotions, etc.), Eucharistic Adoration, Holy Hours and prayer groups.

Liturgical Assistance and Spiritual Life Groups

Choir - The choir assists in leading and promoting worship at 10:30am Mass on Sundays, September through June, and for all major church feasts. Cantors assist in leading song at Mass. The choir meets every Wednesday at 7:30pm in the choir room to practice. It welcomes all that like to sing. Contact: Director of Music Gabriel Pellegrino 671-7933

Altar Servers - assist the priest in many ways at Mass. They present the bread and wine to the priest, ring the bells at the Consecration, and hold the paten at Communion time. Practicing Catholics from fourth-graders to adults are invited to be altar servers. Training is provided. Contact: Pastoral Associate Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317

Lectors and Commentators - proclaim the Scriptures at Mass. Lectors are assigned at the weekend Mass they regularly attend. In order to proclaim God's Word effectively, preparation and practice are required. Practicing, fully initiated Catholics are invited to be lector/commentators. Complete training (including practice with the microphone) is provided.
Contact: Pastoral Associate Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317

Communion Ministers At Mass - nourish the Body of Christ with the precious Body and Blood of Jesus. The General Instruction of the Roman Missal states, "if such ordinary ministers of Holy Communion are not present, the priest may call upon EXTRAORDINARY MINISTERS to help him." Communion Ministers are assigned at the weekend Mass they regularly attend. Practicing, fully initiated Catholics are invited to be Communion Ministers. Training is provided. Contact: Pastoral Associate
Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317

Ushers - welcome and direct people to seats, take up the collection and facilitate Communion lines. They also record weekend mass attendance, invite people to bring up the offertory gifts, and distribute the parish bulletin. Ushers are needed on weekend Masses, holiday Masses, and for Masses involving the Sacraments, for example Confirmation and First Communion. Edward Matuck 671-4564

Children's Liturgy - At the 10:30AM Mass each Sunday during the school year, volunteers share the Readings and Gospel of the Day with children ages 5-7. There is time for age-appropriate discussion. Children return to church after the homily with a fun handout about the readings to take home. Contact: Pastoral Associate Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317

Eucharistic Adoration - is a ministry of prayer before the Blessed Sacrament. An hour is assigned to one or more adorers who make a commitment to that hour. There are 11 hourly coordinators who ensure that someone is with Our Lord at each assigned hour. Adorers may pray the rosary, make the Stations of the Cross, read scriptural books, or use their own prayer guides. A book of petitions is on the sign-in table. Adorers are encouraged to pray for those requests as well as their own. All are welcome: Catholic and non-Catholic, Holy Spirit parishioners and non-parishioners. Contact: Eucharistic Adoration Division Coordinator for a schedule of daily hours. Joan Poore 377-3463

Children's Holy Half-Hour - Eucharistic adoration for families with children of all ages that can include praying the Rosary or Stations of the Cross, scripture readings, stories about the lives of the Saints and quiet time. The leading family decides content of the half-hour. Saturdays at 3:00-3:30pm. Cristine Rembold 671-5186

Rosary - Monday through Friday at 8:30am (before the 9am Mass) and immediately after the 8am Mass on Saturday. They are also held on Wednesdays at 7pm for May and October devotions.

Holy Hour and Benediction - Friday Holy Hour is 8-9pm, Sunday night prayer and Benediction is from 8:45-9pm, and there are nightly closing prayers after Solemn Adoration at 9:55pm.

First Friday and First Saturday Confessions/Devotions - Confessions: first Thursday of the month, 3:30-4:30pm. Devotions to the Sacred Heart of Jesus are held after the first Friday 9am Mass and devotions to the Immaculate Heart of Mary are held after the first Saturday 8am Mass.

Men's Prayer and Witness Breakfast - gives an opportunity to meet other Catholic men of our area who try to live their faith. Scheduled for a Saturday, morning begins with 8am Mass followed by breakfast in the parish center - cost is $5.00 (or $3.00 for boys twelve and under), then a featured speaker. Many have said it was of great encouragement to share what their Catholic faith has come to mean to them. We conclude with prayer around 10:15am. But, afterwards there's ample time to meet, talk and pray further. Everyone is welcome (Catholic or non-Catholic).
Tom Ryan 671-2736

The Notre Dame Retreat House in Canandaigua each fall.
Men's Retreat – Dave Swift Jr. 671-3227
Women's Retreat – Mary Cantatore 671-6047
