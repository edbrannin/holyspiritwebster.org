PARISH LEADERSHIP

Parish Council - The Council is made up of twelve members (Four are elected annually, in June, and hold a 3-year term) plus the Pastor and staff. Meetings are held at 7:15pm on the third Tuesday of each month. All parishioners are encouraged to attend. The Council establishes goals, objectives and programs to encourage participation of all parish members in spiritual and temporal activities to fulfill the pastoral mission of the Church. The Council seeks monthly input from every parishioner before each Sunday Mass. A Suggestion Box and pictorial Directory of Council members and officers are located in the Gathering Area.
Chuck Mertz 545-4171

Parish Council Standing Committees

Liturgy Committee - develops and maintains a program of liturgical worship, working in conjunction with the PASTOR, the PASTORAL ASSOCIATE, and the DIRECTOR OF MUSIC. Responsibilities will include recruitment, training, and evaluation of lectors (commentators), Communion Ministers, ushers, altar servers, and sacristans. Consideration should also be given to the Rite of Christian initiation of Adults (RCIA), other special worship programs, music resources, and the Church environment. Deacon Brian Mahoney 671-8317

Finance Committee - prepares the annual budget and monitors parish expenditures in support of the parish mission. Alice Winter 671-2793

Buildings and Property Maintenance Committee - has charge of the care and maintenance of all church property including the rectory.
Alice Winter 671-2793

Grounds and Landscapes Committee - takes care of church grounds including snow removal in the winter and flowers and gardens for the summer. Jim Bielan 671-4336

Social Ministry Committee - directs Holy Spirit participation in community ecumenical and social welfare programs. It seeks to improve race relations, particularly on a local basis, assisting the needy and promote ecumenical spirit by participation in programs with other denominations. Its attention is toward community problems, such as pollution, drug abuse, standard housing, etc.

Evangelization Committee - reaches out to inactive members of the parish and will help to make the parish visible in the community.
Tony Ferrera 671-5680

Faith Formation and Christian Education Committee - assists in the development of religious education programs for children, youth and adults and aids in the recruitment and training of teachers.
Tony Ferrera 671-5680

Social Events Committee - chairs and plans parish community events for each season. Debbie Pellegrino 671-0694

Stewardship Committee - will function in direct communication with the Pastor and Pastoral Council to plan, coordinate, and implement an ongoing stewardship process and resource development plan for the parish. Carol Rutigliano 266-3881

Communications Committee - will foster and enhance communication and collaboration between the faith communities within the Parish of the Holy Spirit and all external organizations with which the Parish exchanges information or operates on collaborative efforts.
Maria Pellegrino 671-7933

Webster/Penfield Pastoral Planning Council - Parish leaders from Holy Spirit, Holy Trinity, St. Rita, and St. Paul participate on an inter-parish and diocesan level to periodically monitor parish success in achieving more universal goals. 2 staff representatives, 1 parishioner- at-large and 2 parish council members are appointed from each parish to serve 2-year term. Meetings are usually Monday evenings four times a year, more during Pastoral Planning phases. Shari Magagnoli 671-5520
