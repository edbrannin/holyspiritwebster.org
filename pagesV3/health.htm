Your Guide to Lowering High Blood Pressure:
Healthy Eating
Research has shown that following a healthy eating plan can both reduce the risk of developing high blood pressure and lower an already elevated blood pressure.

A healthy eating plan is one that emphasizes fruits, vegetables, and lowfat dairy foods and is low in saturated fat, total fat, and cholesterol. This plan would also include whole grains, poultry, fish, and nuts and has reduced amounts of fats, red meat, sweets and sugared beverages.
Healthy eating would include a low sodium diet. Most Americans consume more salt than they need. The current recommendation is to consume less than 2.4 grams (2,400 milligrams) of sodium a day. That equals 6 grams (about 1 teaspoon) of table salt a day. A lower sodium diet can keep blood pressure from rising and help blood pressure medicines work better..

Limit Alcohol Intake:
Drinking too much alcohol can raise blood pressure. It also can harm the liver, brain, and heart. Alcoholic drinks also contain calories, which matter if you are trying to lose weight. If you drink alcoholic beverages, have only a moderate amount - one drink a day for women and two drinks a day for men. What counts as a drink? A drink is, 12 oz. of beer, or 5 oz. of wine, or 1½ oz. of 80-proof whiskey

Quit Smoking:
Smoking injures blood vessel walls and speeds up the process of hardening of the arteries. This applies even to filtered cigarettes. So even though it does not cause high blood pressure, smoking is bad for anyone, especially those with high blood pressure. If you smoke, quit. If you don’t smoke, don’t start. Once you quit, your risk of having a heart attack is reduced after the first year. So you have a lot to gain by quitting.

The Health Ministry is pleased to have brought you this series of information on high blood pressure. We invite and encourage you to stop in and have your blood pressure checked when screenings are offered at your parish. You can also check the monthly calendar to see which weekend each of the parishes offers blood pressure screenings.

WPCC Health Ministry’s “Spotlight on Wellness”
Stay Hydrated All Day Long
You probably know the health benefits of drinking plenty of water, but actually not drinking enough of it can lead to dehydration and fatigue. If you need to increase the amount of water you drink: Take water breaks instead of coffee breaks; skip high calorie or sweetened beverages which promote cavities and weight gain Keep a bottle of water with you always Drink water, before, during and after physical activity Add a splash of fruit juice if you don’t like the taste of water Don’t wait until you are thirsty to drink water; stay hydrated all day long!

Get Active, Stay Active
Along with a well-balanced diet, exercise is important for losing weight and maintaining overall health. With planning, you can easily fit 30-60 minutes of aerobic activity into your routine most days of the week. Aerobic activity includes walking, bike riding, dancing, and skating. Decide which activities you enjoy and look at your schedule to see where it can fit in. **If you’re starting from no or little physical activity, check with your physician first but start with 5-10 minute sessions daily. Once you achieve that level, boost it every week by 10 minute increments until you’re up to 30-60 minutes per day. For maximum cardiovascular health, do the activity all at one time instead of breaking it up throughout the day.

 


Advance Care Planning: What is it?
Advance Care Planning is a process of planning for future medical care in case you are unable to make your own decisions. It is a continual process and not merely a document or isolated event and will assist you in preparing for a sudden unexpected illness from which you expect to recover, as well as the dying process and ultimately death.
Advance Care Planning is a gift to you and your family and is important in assuring that your wishes are honored if you are unable to speak for yourself. Directives apply only when the need arises and you are unable to speak for yourself.
Information on Advance Care Planning and Health Care Proxy forms, including a Guide for Catholics, will be available once a month, at your Blood Pressure Screening site beginning the weekend of February 6/7. Please check the bulletin for more information on this topic.

The Advance Care Planning Process
involves the following:

  • Becoming educated about the topic.
  • Exploring, clarifying, and documenting your values, beliefs and goals.
  • Choosing a Spokesperson and an Alternate to work with doctors on your behalf in the event you are unable to speak for yourself.
  • Reviewing your wishes with your Spokesperson, Alternate and those important to you.
  • Completing the NYS Health Care Proxy and Living Will forms that identify your Spokesperson and Alternate and specify your wishes.
  • Reviewing and updating these forms periodically and after major life-altering events.

Conducting ongoing discussions about updates with your Spokesperson and Alternates

Advance Care Planning permits peace of mind for you and your family by reducing uncertainty and helping to avoid confusion and conflict over your care should you become unable to speak for yourself.

FLU GUIDELINES:
As you know, there's been a lot in the news media of late about the flu. As has always been the case if you or members of your family are coughing, sneezing or otherwise ill, it is a good idea to stay home from Mass to recuperate and so as not to infect others. Likewise, you should not drink from the cup during Holy Communion if you are sick; remember that we receive the whole Christ, "body, blood, soul and divinity" when we receive of the Eucharist in either form. During the Sign of Peace, you can offer a smile and little bow rather than a handshake if you feel under the weather or feel especially vulnerable, just as you can refrain from holding hands during the Our Father from similar concerns. For our part, we'll be monitoring the situation in our area carefully through health advisories, and we'll adjust our practices as a faith community if necessary
